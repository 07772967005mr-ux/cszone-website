1.6.1b — Cultural & Environmental Issues
Component 1: Computer Systems  ·  ~18 min

Cultural Issues

Digital divide:

  • The gap between those who have access to computers and the internet and those who don't
  • Causes: wealth, geography, age, disability, education
  • Consequences: those without access cannot participate in digital economy, online education, or government services
  • Global digital divide: developing countries have much lower internet access

Impact of technology on employment:

  • Job displacement: automation replaces routine jobs (factory workers, checkout operators, data entry)
  • New jobs created: software developers, data scientists, cybersecurity analysts
  • Remote working: technology enables working from home — changed work culture
  • Gig economy: platforms like Uber and Deliveroo create flexible but precarious work

Accessibility:

  • Technology should be usable by people with disabilities
  • Screen readers for visually impaired users
  • Voice control, magnification, subtitles
  • Web accessibility standards (WCAG)

Censorship and freedom of information:

  • Some governments restrict internet access (China's Great Firewall)
  • Tension between free speech and preventing harmful content
  • Social media platforms moderate content — decisions about what is acceptable vary

Environmental Issues

Energy consumption of data centres:

  • Data centres consume enormous amounts of electricity (cooling + computing)
  • Streaming video, cloud computing, cryptocurrency mining are particularly energy-intensive
  • Some companies use renewable energy for data centres

E-waste (electronic waste):

  • Discarded electronics contain toxic materials (lead, mercury, cadmium)
  • Short product lifecycles mean more frequent replacement
  • Much e-waste ends up in landfill in developing countries
  • Recycling programmes: responsibly disposing of electronics

Carbon footprint:

  • Manufacturing devices requires significant energy and rare materials
  • Internet traffic has a measurable carbon footprint
  • Cloud computing CAN be more energy efficient than on-site servers (economies of scale)

Benefits of technology for the environment:

  • Smart grids optimise electricity distribution
  • Remote working reduces commuting and office energy use
  • Precision agriculture uses sensors to reduce water and fertiliser waste
  • Monitoring systems track pollution, deforestation, and climate change

Common Mistakes

  • Only stating negatives — examiners want balanced analysis showing benefits too
  • Confusing e-waste and energy consumption — different environmental issues
  • Forgetting the digital divide has economic AND social consequences
  • Saying technology only creates unemployment — it also creates new roles
