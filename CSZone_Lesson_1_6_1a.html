1.6.1a — Ethical & Legal Issues
Component 1: Computer Systems  ·  ~22 min  ·  Free lesson

Ethical Issues in Computing

Computer systems raise significant ethical questions — about privacy, fairness, access, and responsibility. The OCR J277 spec requires understanding of how computing impacts society ethically and legally.

Privacy and personal data:

  • Companies collect vast amounts of personal data (location, purchases, browsing habits)
  • Ethical concerns: is this data being used fairly? Is it shared without consent?
  • Social media companies profile users for targeted advertising
  • Governments may use data for surveillance — balance between security and privacy

Artificial intelligence bias:

  • Facial recognition systems have been shown to be less accurate for darker skin tones
  • Algorithms trained on biased data produce biased outputs
  • Used in recruitment, criminal justice, lending — bias can have real consequences

Intellectual property & copyright:

  • Software and digital content are protected by copyright — illegal to copy without permission
  • Open source software: code is freely available to use, modify, and distribute
  • Piracy: illegal copying and distribution of software, films, music

UK Legislation

Data Protection Act 2018 / UK GDPR

  • Regulates how personal data is collected, stored, and used
  • 8 principles: data must be used fairly/lawfully, for specified purposes, minimally, accurately, not kept longer than necessary, securely, with individual rights respected
  • Enforced by the ICO (Information Commissioner's Office)
  • Individuals have rights: access their data, correct errors, request deletion

Computer Misuse Act 1990

  • Makes it illegal to: access a computer without authorisation (hacking), access with intent to commit further offences, modify computer material without authorisation (installing malware)
  • 3 offences: unauthorised access, unauthorised access to commit crime, unauthorised modification

Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988

  • Protects creative works including software
  • Illegal to copy, distribute, or sell copyrighted software without a licence
  • Piracy of software, films, and music violates this act

Freedom of Information Act 2000

  • Public bodies must publish information and respond to requests for information
  • Promotes transparency of government

Common Mistakes

  • Confusing DPA and CMA — DPA covers personal data; CMA covers unauthorised computer access
  • Not stating the YEAR of legislation — OCR expects the correct year
  • Forgetting the ICO enforces the DPA
  • Saying the CMA only covers "hacking" — it also covers modification (malware installation)
