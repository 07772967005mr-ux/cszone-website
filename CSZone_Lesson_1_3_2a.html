1.3.2a — Wired & Wireless Networks
Component 1: Computer Systems  ·  ~20 min

Wired vs Wireless — Overview

Networks can use physical cables (wired) or radio waves (wireless) to transmit data. Each has distinct advantages and disadvantages.

Wired Connections

Ethernet (copper cable):

  • Uses twisted pair copper cables (Cat5e, Cat6, etc.)
  • Typical speeds: 100 Mbps (Fast Ethernet) to 10 Gbps (10GbE)
  • Reliable — not affected by interference from other devices
  • More secure — harder to intercept than wireless
  • Requires physical cable installation

Fibre optic cable:

  • Transmits data as pulses of light through glass/plastic fibres
  • Very high speeds (Gbps–Tbps range)
  • Not affected by electrical interference (EMI)
  • Ideal for long-distance and high-bandwidth links
  • More expensive to install than copper

Wireless Connections (Wi-Fi)

Wi-Fi:

  • Uses radio waves to transmit data
  • Devices connect via a Wireless Access Point (WAP)
  • Portable — devices can move while connected
  • Speed and range affected by walls, distance, interference
  • Less secure than wired (signals can be intercepted if not encrypted)

Bluetooth:

  • Short-range wireless connection (typically up to 10 metres)
  • Used for peripheral devices (keyboards, mice, headphones)
  • Lower power consumption than Wi-Fi
  • Personal Area Network (PAN)

Factors Affecting Network Performance

FactorEffect on performance
BandwidthHigher bandwidth = more data transferred per second
LatencyDelay in data transmission — lower is better
InterferenceOther wireless devices, walls, microwaves reduce Wi-Fi signal
Number of usersMore devices sharing a connection = less bandwidth each
DistanceWi-Fi weakens with distance; wired performance is consistent
Transmission mediumFibre > copper cable > wireless for speed and reliability

Common Mistakes

  • Saying wireless is always better — wired is faster and more reliable
  • Confusing bandwidth and speed — bandwidth is the maximum capacity; actual speed may be lower
  • Forgetting that Wi-Fi signals can be intercepted — always emphasise encryption (WPA2/WPA3)
  • Saying fibre optic uses electrical signals — it uses light

Wired vs Wireless Comparison
